# Cost Governance — Best Practices

## Common scenarios
- Establishing organization-wide spend policies, budgets, and guardrails across many AWS accounts        → Cost Governance, Account Structure
- Preventing cost shock from unintentional usage (wrong Region, unapproved services, runaway resources)        → Guardrails, Anomaly Detection
- Attributing shared cloud spend to teams, products, or cost centers for showback/chargeback        → Cost Allocation
- Building a cost-aware culture where engineering and finance share accountability for spend        → Culture & Accountability

## Account Structure & Organization
- **[multi-account design]** Map your AWS account structure (via AWS Organizations and OUs) to your organization's business units, environments, or workloads — this is the primary mechanism for allocating and isolating costs. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/wellarchitected/latest/framework/cost_govern_usage_account_structure.html)
- **[consolidated billing]** Use consolidated billing under a single management account to aggregate usage across member accounts — this maximizes volume discounts and shared commitment-discount utilization (Savings Plans/Reserved Instances) while preserving per-account cost visibility. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/wellarchitected/latest/framework/cost_govern_usage_account_structure.html)
- **[resource placement]** Keep the management (payer) account free of workload resources — place all workloads in member accounts so the management account is used purely for governance and billing. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/wellarchitected/latest/framework/cost_govern_usage_account_structure.html)
- **[automated setup]** Use AWS Control Tower to provision and govern a multi-account landing zone consistently, rather than manually creating and configuring accounts one at a time. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/wellarchitected/latest/framework/cost_govern_usage_account_structure.html)

## Policies, Guardrails & Controls
- **[policy-first]** Develop cost governance policies based on your organization's actual requirements (goals, targets, account structure, roles) before implementing technical controls — controls without agreed policy create friction and get bypassed. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/wellarchitected/latest/framework/cost-02.html)
- **[service control policies]** Use AWS Organizations Service Control Policies (SCPs) to restrict which Regions, instance types, or services can be used by member accounts — this stops unplanned or forgotten deployments (for example, in an unused Region) before they generate cost. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/wellarchitected/latest/framework/cost_govern_usage_controls.html)
- **[approval workflow]** Pair restrictive SCPs with a documented request-and-approval process so teams can get temporary access to denied services or Regions instead of being permanently blocked — undocumented denials create shadow workarounds. [doc](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/aws-cloud-financial-management/cost-control-blog-series-3-how-to-handle-cost-shock/)
- **[least privilege]** Use IAM policies and roles to define who can create, modify, or delete resources and in which Regions, minimizing the chance of resources being created outside policy. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/wellarchitected/latest/framework/cost_govern_usage_controls.html)
- **[protect the guardrails]** Apply an SCP at the organization root that denies modification or deletion of Budgets actions and Cost Anomaly Detection configuration by anyone outside a designated admin role — this prevents teams from silently disabling the cost controls placed on them. [doc](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/mt/codify-your-best-practices-using-service-control-policies-part-2/)
- **[service quotas]** Actively manage AWS service quotas so they track actual need rather than being left at defaults or over-inflated — tightly maintained quotas reduce the chance of large-scale unplanned resource creation. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/wellarchitected/latest/framework/cost_govern_usage_controls.html)

## Budgets & Automated Enforcement
- **[layered budgets]** Configure AWS Budgets at multiple levels — overall account, then finer-grained budgets by linked account, service, Region, or tag — rather than a single aggregate budget, so overspend can be localized quickly. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/wellarchitected/latest/framework/cost_govern_usage_controls.html)
- **[shared alerting]** Send budget notifications to an email distribution list or SNS topic rather than an individual's inbox, so alerts survive personnel changes and reach the whole responsible team. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/wellarchitected/latest/framework/cost_govern_usage_controls.html)
- **[forecast + actual]** Configure both actual-spend and forecasted-spend budget alerts so you're notified before a threshold is breached, not only after. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/wellarchitected/latest/framework/cost_govern_usage_controls.html)
- **[budget actions]** Use AWS Budgets Actions to automatically (or with manual approval) apply an IAM deny policy, apply an SCP, or stop targeted EC2/RDS instances when a threshold is exceeded — this converts a passive alert into an enforced guardrail. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/cost-management/latest/userguide/budgets-controls.html)
- **[non-prod focus]** Apply disruptive budget actions (stopping instances, restrictive SCPs) primarily to non-production, sandbox, or development accounts, and coordinate carefully with owners before applying them to production. [doc](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/mt/manage-cost-overruns-part-1/)
- **[budget governance]** Restrict who can modify or delete existing budgets and budget actions through IAM/SCP so a team cannot quietly widen its own guardrails after they've been set. [doc](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/mt/codify-your-best-practices-using-service-control-policies-part-2/)

## Anomaly Detection & Spend Monitoring
- **[continuous monitoring]** Enable AWS Cost Anomaly Detection alongside AWS Budgets — budgets catch known thresholds while anomaly detection uses machine learning to catch unexpected spend patterns you didn't think to budget for. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/wellarchitected/latest/framework/cost_govern_usage_controls.html)
- **[monitor scope]** Create Cost Anomaly Detection monitors scoped to AWS services, member accounts, cost allocation tags, or Cost Categories so alerts can be routed to the team actually responsible for the root cause. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/cost-management/latest/userguide/manage-ad.html)
- **[threshold tuning]** Set a dollar-impact alert threshold deliberately (for example, alert only above a meaningful absolute or percentage impact) to avoid alert fatigue from normal seasonal or growth-driven variation. [doc](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/aws-cloud-financial-management/cost-control-blog-series-3-how-to-handle-cost-shock/)
- **[detection latency]** Account for Cost Anomaly Detection's dependency on Cost Explorer data (up to 24 hours delay) and the 10-day historical baseline required for new services — don't expect same-hour detection for brand-new spend categories. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/cost-management/latest/userguide/manage-ad.html)
- **[chat integration]** Route anomaly and budget alerts into Slack or Chime via Amazon SNS and AWS Chatbot so operational teams see spend anomalies in the same channels they already monitor. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/cost-management/latest/userguide/manage-ad.html)

## Cost Allocation, Showback & Chargeback
- **[tagging strategy]** Define and enforce an organization-wide resource tagging taxonomy (cost center, business unit, project, environment) before you need granular cost attribution — retrofitting tags onto untagged historical resources is far harder. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/whitepapers/latest/tagging-best-practices/building-a-cost-allocation-strategy.html)
- **[activate cost tags]** Activate both user-defined and AWS-generated cost allocation tags in the management account's Billing console — tags are invisible to Cost Explorer, Budgets, and CUR until explicitly activated. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/awsaccountbilling/latest/aboutv2/custom-tags.html)
- **[enforce compliance]** Use AWS Organizations tag policies and the AWS Config `required-tags` managed rule to enforce the tagging standard, and consider automatically flagging or terminating non-compliant resources in non-production accounts. [doc](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/apn/reducing-the-cost-of-managing-multiple-aws-accounts-using-aws-control-tower/)
- **[retroactive cleanup]** Use AWS Tag Editor to find and remediate untagged resources created before your tagging policy existed, since tag policies and SCPs only apply going forward. [doc](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/aws-cloud-financial-management/gs-create-and-enforce-your-tagging-strategy-for-more-granular-cost-visibility/)
- **[cost categories]** Use AWS Cost Categories to group accounts and tags ("meta-tagging") into business-meaningful buckets, enabling analysis in Cost Explorer, Budgets, and Cost and Usage Report without re-tagging every resource. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/whitepapers/latest/tagging-best-practices/building-a-cost-allocation-strategy.html)
- **[showback before chargeback]** Implement showback reporting (attributing cost to teams/business units without financial transfer) as the prerequisite step before attempting formal chargeback — you need trusted attribution before you can bill internally on it. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/wellarchitected/latest/cost-optimization-pillar/cost_type_size_number_resources_shared.html)
- **[shared cost allocation]** For shared or untaggable resources, decide explicitly on an allocation model (even split, proportional to spend, or fixed cost) and use split-charge Cost Categories to calculate the allocation consistently each month. [doc](https://aws.amazon.com/solutions/guidance/cloud-financial-management-on-aws/)
- **[per-account billing]** When business units need fully separate invoices or custom pricing, use AWS Billing Conductor to create billing groups and custom line items rather than relying solely on tag-based reporting. [doc](https://aws.amazon.com/solutions/guidance/cloud-financial-management-on-aws/)

## Cost-Aware Culture & Accountability
- **[shared responsibility]** Treat cost as a shared responsibility between engineering and finance rather than solely a finance function — give engineers direct visibility into the cost impact of their architecture decisions. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/wellarchitected/latest/cost-optimization-pillar/cost_cloud_financial_management_culture.html)
- **[embed in change management]** Include a cost-impact assessment in your change management process so the financial effect of infrastructure changes is quantified proactively, not discovered after the invoice arrives. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/wellarchitected/latest/cost-optimization-pillar/cost_cloud_financial_management_cost_awareness.html)
- **[incident-style response]** Reuse existing incident management processes to investigate and root-cause cost or usage anomalies, the same way you would an availability incident. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/wellarchitected/latest/cost-optimization-pillar/cost_cloud_financial_management_cost_awareness.html)
- **[visible dashboards]** Maintain a highly visible cost-and-usage dashboard (tailored per audience — engineering vs. management) so current spend levels are ambient knowledge rather than a monthly surprise. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/wellarchitected/2024-06-27/framework/cost_monitor_usage_config_tools.html)
- **[training]** Extend existing training and certification programs to include continuous cost-awareness training so teams can self-manage cost and usage rather than depending on central FinOps review. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/wellarchitected/latest/cost-optimization-pillar/cost_cloud_financial_management_cost_awareness.html)
- **[reporting]** Configure AWS Budgets Reports to deliver a consolidated view of up to 50 budgets on a daily, weekly, or monthly cadence directly to stakeholders instead of requiring manual dashboard checks. [doc](https://aws.amazon.com/solutions/guidance/cloud-financial-management-on-aws/)

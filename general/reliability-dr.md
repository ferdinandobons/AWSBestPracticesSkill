# Reliability & Disaster Recovery — Best Practices

## Common scenarios
- Defining acceptable downtime and data-loss targets before choosing an architecture        → Recovery Planning & Objectives
- Building a highly available foundation within a Region before considering multi-Region        → Multi-AZ & Multi-Region Architecture
- Protecting against ransomware, accidental deletion, and Region-wide disasters        → Backup & Data Protection
- Automatically detecting and routing away from unhealthy resources        → Failover & Health Checks
- Preventing cascading failures and retry storms between distributed components        → Fault Isolation & Graceful Degradation
- Proving that recovery procedures actually work before a real incident happens        → Resilience Testing & Validation
- Rolling out changes safely so bad deployments don't become outages        → Change Management & Automation
- Avoiding outages caused by hitting an undocumented account or resource ceiling        → Capacity & Service Quotas

## Recovery Planning & Objectives
- **[RTO/RPO]** Define a recovery time objective (RTO) and recovery point objective (RPO) for every workload based on business impact analysis, not arbitrary or default values. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/wellarchitected/2023-10-03/framework/rel_planning_for_recovery_objective_defined_recovery.html)
- **[RTO/RPO]** Group workloads into tiers with shared recovery objectives (e.g., near-zero RPO/15-minute RTO for tier-1, hours for tier-3) so the DR strategy investment matches criticality. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/whitepapers/latest/disaster-recovery-of-on-premises-applications-to-aws/recovery-objectives.html)
- **[Cost tradeoff]** Evaluate the cost and operational complexity of each RTO/RPO target against the actual business requirement — a lower RTO/RPO is only worth it if the workload genuinely needs it. [doc](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/mt/establishing-rpo-and-rto-targets-for-cloud-applications/)
- **[Strategy selection]** Choose a DR strategy — backup and restore, pilot light, warm standby, or multi-site active/active — based on your RTO/RPO, in increasing order of cost and complexity and decreasing order of recovery time. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/wellarchitected/latest/reliability-pillar/rel_planning_for_recovery_disaster_recovery.html)
- **[Assessment]** Use AWS Resilience Hub to assess a workload's architecture against its assigned resiliency policy and surface single points of failure and unmapped dependencies before they cause an outage. [doc](https://aws.amazon.com/resilience-hub/)

## Multi-AZ & Multi-Region Architecture
- **[Foundation]** Deploy workload components across multiple Availability Zones by default — a workload can only reach "four nines" availability if it is not confined to a single AZ. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/wellarchitected/2023-10-03/framework/rel_fault_isolation_select_location.html)
- **[Scoping]** Reserve multi-Region architectures for workloads with extreme resilience requirements rather than defaulting to them — a multi-Region design when a multi-AZ design would suffice adds unnecessary cost and complexity. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/wellarchitected/2023-10-03/framework/rel_fault_isolation_select_location.html)
- **[Baseline first]** Establish a resilient multi-AZ baseline before adding multi-Region replication — a workload that cannot tolerate a single AZ failure will simply replicate its vulnerabilities across Regions. [doc](https://aws.amazon.com/isv/resources/5-essential-strategies-for-aws-multi-region-resilience/)
- **[Managed failover]** Use managed multi-AZ capabilities such as Amazon RDS Multi-AZ, Aurora Multi-AZ, and Auto Scaling groups spread across AZs so common hardware, power, and networking failures are absorbed without custom failover tooling. [doc](https://aws.amazon.com/isv/resources/5-essential-strategies-for-aws-multi-region-resilience/)
- **[Gray failures]** Build additional observability for "gray failures" (partial, hard-to-detect degradations) in zonal-service workloads, since these are not automatically mitigated by multi-AZ configurations the way clean hardware failures are. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/whitepapers/latest/advanced-multi-az-resilience-patterns/advanced-multi-az-resilience-patterns.html)
- **[Traffic locality]** Prefer intra-AZ communication and AZ-aware load balancing when distributing resources across AZs or Regions to reduce cross-AZ data-transfer exposure and latency. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/wellarchitected/latest/video-streaming-advertising-lens/advrel03-bp01.html)

## Backup & Data Protection
- **[Centralized policy]** Centralize backup policies across accounts and services with AWS Backup rather than building per-service ad hoc backup scripts, so retention, scheduling, and compliance are consistently enforced. [doc](https://aws.amazon.com/backup/features/)
- **[Encryption]** Encrypt backup vaults with AWS KMS keys that are independent from the keys used to encrypt the source resources, so a compromise of production keys does not also compromise backup data. [doc](https://aws.amazon.com/backup/features/)
- **[Cross-Region/account]** Copy backups across AWS Regions and accounts from a central console to meet disaster recovery objectives and to ensure a compromised primary account or Region cannot also destroy the backup copy. [doc](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/security/use-backups-to-recover-from-security-incidents/)
- **[Immutability]** Enable AWS Backup Vault Lock in compliance mode for critical backup vaults so that no user, including the root user, can delete or shorten retention on backups during the lock period — a key defense against ransomware. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/prescriptive-guidance/latest/security-best-practices/safeguard.html)
- **[Immutability]** Use the Vault Lock cooling-off period to test the lock configuration against real workloads before it becomes permanent, since compliance-mode locks cannot later be loosened. [doc](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/storage/protecting-data-with-aws-backup-vault-lock/)
- **[Governance]** Monitor Vault Lock compliance status across all accounts in an organization to close gaps where backup vaults lack immutability controls. [doc](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/infrastructure-and-automation/monitor-aws-backup-vault-lock-compliance-across-your-organization/)
- **[Continuous backup]** Use automated or continuous backups with point-in-time recovery to lower RPO for a backup-and-restore DR strategy instead of relying solely on periodic snapshots. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/wellarchitected/latest/reliability-pillar/rel_planning_for_recovery_disaster_recovery.html)

## Failover & Health Checks
- **[Health checks]** Configure Route 53 health checks with intervals and failure thresholds matched to the criticality of the monitored resource — shorter intervals detect failure faster but increase check load and cost. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/Route53/latest/DeveloperGuide/best-practices-healthchecks.html)
- **[Health checks]** Use multiple health-checker Regions for critical resources so a localized network issue in one health-checker Region does not trigger a false failover. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/Route53/latest/DeveloperGuide/best-practices-healthchecks.html)
- **[Alarming]** Integrate Route 53 health checks with CloudWatch alarms so failures and recoveries trigger notifications tied into incident response, not just silent DNS changes. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/Route53/latest/DeveloperGuide/best-practices-healthchecks.html)
- **[DNS failover]** Use Route 53 failover routing policies with health checks to automatically route traffic to a healthy secondary resource, relying on the Route 53 data plane rather than the control plane for the failover decision to avoid dependency on an impaired Region. [doc](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/networking-and-content-delivery/creating-disaster-recovery-mechanisms-using-amazon-route-53/)
- **[Forced failover]** Do not depend on the Route 53 health-check API to force a manual failover during an incident — automatic health-check-driven failover does not require this control-plane dependency and is more resilient. [doc](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/networking-and-content-delivery/creating-disaster-recovery-mechanisms-using-amazon-route-53/)
- **[CDN + DNS]** Combine CloudFront origin failover (fast, latency-sensitive) with Route 53 DNS failover (more stable, slower) for layered protection of web application availability. [doc](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/networking-and-content-delivery/improve-web-application-availability-with-cloudfront-and-route53-hybrid-origin-failover/)
- **[Bounded work]** Design health-checking and failover pipelines to perform bounded work regardless of runtime conditions, so a mass endpoint failure (e.g., an entire AZ) does not create a processing backlog that delays failover. [doc](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/architecture/doing-constant-work-to-avoid-failures/)

## Fault Isolation & Graceful Degradation
- **[Retries]** Implement exponential backoff with jitter for client retries, and cap both the backoff delay and the maximum retry count, rather than retrying immediately and aggressively. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/wellarchitected/latest/reliability-pillar/rel_mitigate_interaction_failure_limit_retries.html)
- **[Retries]** Verify that a backoff/retry mechanism actually exists and behaves as expected — never assume an SDK or library already implements it correctly without testing. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/wellarchitected/2022-03-31/framework/rel_mitigate_interaction_failure_limit_retries.html)
- **[Retries]** Retry only at a single layer of the application stack — retrying independently at multiple layers compounds attempts and can itself cause a retry storm. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/wellarchitected/latest/reliability-pillar/rel_mitigate_interaction_failure_limit_retries.html)
- **[Retries]** Avoid retrying non-idempotent operations, since retries can cause duplicated side effects (e.g., double-charged payments) rather than a safe recovery. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/wellarchitected/latest/reliability-pillar/rel_mitigate_interaction_failure_limit_retries.html)
- **[Read/write isolation]** Isolate database reads and writes (e.g., via read replicas and connection pooling/proxying) so latency or contention on writes does not cascade into read-path failures. [doc](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/architecture/a-multi-dimensional-approach-helps-you-proactively-prepare-for-failures-part-1-application-layer/)
- **[Graceful degradation]** Inform callers via headers or metadata when to retry during degraded performance or fail-fast scenarios, instead of leaving clients to guess. [doc](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/compute/building-well-architected-serverless-applications-building-in-resiliency-part-1/)

## Resilience Testing & Validation
- **[Chaos engineering]** Run fault injection experiments with AWS Fault Injection Service regularly, including as part of CI/CD pipelines, to validate that a workload actually recovers the way it was designed to. [doc](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/architecture/chaos-testing-with-aws-fault-injection-simulator-and-aws-codepipeline/)
- **[Chaos engineering]** Configure CloudWatch alarms as stop conditions on fault injection experiments so an experiment automatically rolls back if it causes unexpected impact beyond the intended blast radius. [doc](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/architecture/verify-the-resilience-of-your-workloads-using-chaos-engineering/)
- **[Combined testing]** Combine chaos engineering (unexpected faults) with resilience testing (expected, known failure scenarios) to build confidence that the workload both survives component failure and recovers from unexpected disruption. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/wellarchitected/2025-02-25/framework/rel_testing_resiliency_failure_injection_resiliency.html)
- **[Game days]** Conduct regular game days that simulate real disruption scenarios with all stakeholders informed in advance, and roll back immediately if negative impact is observed, remedying issues found before the next attempt. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/wellarchitected/latest/framework/rel_testing_resiliency_game_days_resiliency.html)
- **[Feedback loop]** Document and incorporate lessons learned from every game day into a systematic feedback loop that improves systems, processes, and team response capability over time. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/wellarchitected/latest/framework/rel_testing_resiliency_game_days_resiliency.html)
- **[DR-specific testing]** Periodically test the DR plan itself against its defined RTO and RPO — a DR plan that has never been exercised should not be trusted to meet its stated objectives during a real event. [doc](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/apn/testing-aws-gameday-with-the-aws-well-architected-framework/)
- **[Backup accessibility]** Verify that backups remain accessible and restorable even under the disaster scenario being planned for (e.g., total loss of the primary account) — a backup stored only in the same account/Region it protects may be unusable exactly when it's needed. [doc](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/apn/testing-aws-gameday-with-the-aws-well-architected-framework-remediation/)
- **[Continuous assessment]** Re-run AWS Resilience Hub assessments whenever the application changes, and consider automatic daily assessment, so architectural drift away from resiliency policy is caught early. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/help-panel/resilience-hub/latest/console/resiliency-policies-myApp.html)

## Change Management & Automation
- **[Automated rollback]** Integrate automated testing and automated rollback into the CI/CD pipeline so a failed deployment reverts to the last known-good state without waiting on manual intervention. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/wellarchitected/2023-10-03/framework/ops_mit_deploy_risks_auto_testing_and_rollback.html)
- **[Small releases]** Architect systems to be updated with smaller, independent releases rather than large bulk changes, since bulk changes are difficult to reverse cleanly when a deployment fails. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/wellarchitected/2023-10-03/framework/ops_mit_deploy_risks_auto_testing_and_rollback.html)
- **[Infrastructure as code]** Deploy applications and infrastructure using AWS CloudFormation or AWS CDK to ensure consistent, repeatable deployments and to reduce recovery time by allowing infrastructure to be reprovisioned automatically during a DR event. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/whitepapers/latest/aws-caf-operations-perspective/change-and-release-management.html)
- **[Automated patching]** Automate OS and application patch deployment with rollback (for example, via AWS Systems Manager Patch Manager) so routine patching does not become a manual, error-prone, risky change. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/whitepapers/latest/aws-caf-operations-perspective/change-and-release-management.html)

## Capacity & Service Quotas
- **[Awareness]** Track service quotas for every AWS service a workload depends on, including Region-specific limits, using Service Quotas and AWS Trusted Advisor rather than discovering the limit during a scaling event. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/wellarchitected/2023-10-03/framework/rel_manage_service_limits_aware_quotas_and_constraints.html)
- **[Proactive monitoring]** Set CloudWatch alarms on throttling and quota-usage metrics to identify approaching limits before they cause service disruption or degradation. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/deadline-cloud/latest/userguide/assistant-service-quotas.html)
- **[Regular review]** Reassess service quota usage on a regular basis, since quotas that were adequate at launch can be exhausted by traffic growth or an Auto Scaling event, causing unexpected disruption. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/wellarchitected/2023-10-03/framework/rel_manage_service_limits_aware_quotas_and_constraints.html)
- **[Resource constraints]** Distinguish account-level service quotas from per-resource service constraints (such as storage size or IOPS limits), and engineer against both, since either can degrade or disrupt a workload if reached unexpectedly. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/wellarchitected/2023-10-03/framework/rel_manage_service_limits_aware_quotas_and_constraints.html)

# Amazon Detective — Best Practices

## Common scenarios
- Root-cause investigation of GuardDuty findings and suspicious activity        → Security, Operational Excellence
- Multi-account, organization-wide security investigation from a central account        → Security, Operational Excellence
- Investigating EC2, EKS/Kubernetes, and IAM role/user behavior over time        → Security, Reliability
- Tracking and forecasting the cost of ingested investigation data        → Cost Optimization

## 🔒 Security
- **[administrator account]** Only invite AWS accounts that you actually oversee into your Detective behavior graph — an administrator account can see all findings and data from its member accounts, so inviting untrusted accounts exposes sensitive security information. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/detective/latest/userguide/security-best-practices.html)
- **[IAM]** Limit who holds the `AmazonDetectiveFullAccess` policy, since it lets a principal manage member accounts, tag the behavior graph, and view all findings across every member account — apply least privilege for behavior-graph administration. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/detective/latest/userguide/security-best-practices.html)
- **[member accounts]** Before accepting a behavior-graph invitation, validate the inviting administrator account's AWS account identifier and confirm it has a legitimate reason to monitor your security data — prevents an unrelated or malicious account from ingesting your CloudTrail, VPC Flow Log, and GuardDuty data. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/detective/latest/userguide/security-best-practices.html)
- **[IAM]** Start from AWS managed policies for Detective and move toward customer-managed, least-privilege policies scoped to specific actions and resources as your use cases stabilize — reduces the permissions available if credentials are compromised. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/detective/latest/userguide/security_iam_id-based-policy-examples.html)
- **[IAM]** Add IAM policy conditions (for example, requiring SSL/TLS or restricting access to calls made through a specific AWS service) and validate policies with IAM Access Analyzer — catches overly permissive statements before they are attached. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/detective/latest/userguide/security_iam_id-based-policy-examples.html)
- **[IAM]** Require multi-factor authentication (MFA) for IAM users and any root user access, and add MFA conditions to policies that call sensitive Detective API operations — adds a control against credential theft. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/detective/latest/userguide/security_iam_id-based-policy-examples.html)
- **[data protection]** Set up individual users through IAM Identity Center or IAM rather than sharing credentials, and enable AWS CloudTrail logging of API and user activity for accounts that use Detective — supports auditability of who accessed investigation data. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/detective/latest/userguide/data-protection.html)
- **[data protection]** Never place confidential or sensitive information (such as customer email addresses) into tags or free-form text fields when working with Detective — this data can end up in billing or diagnostic logs. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/detective/latest/userguide/data-protection.html)
- **[network]** Require TLS 1.2 (prefer TLS 1.3) with cipher suites that support perfect forward secrecy (such as ECDHE) for any client calling the Detective API — protects investigation data and findings in transit. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/detective/latest/userguide/infrastructure-security.html)
- **[network]** Use an interface VPC endpoint (AWS PrivateLink) for Detective, with private DNS enabled, so API calls from within your VPC do not need to traverse the public internet — reduces network exposure for investigation traffic. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/detective/latest/userguide/detective-security-vpc-endpoints-privatelink.html)
- **[multi-account]** Align your Detective delegated administrator account with your existing GuardDuty and Security Hub delegated administrator accounts — enables seamless pivoting between findings and centralizes security-tooling access management. [doc](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/security/simplify-setup-of-amazon-detective-with-aws-organizations/)
- **[detection integration]** Enroll accounts in GuardDuty for at least 48 hours before enabling Detective, and enable GuardDuty even though it is not a strict prerequisite — it substantially enhances Detective's visualizations and finding-driven investigations. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/prescriptive-guidance/latest/security-reference-architecture/security-tooling.html)

## 🛡️ Reliability
- **[training period]** Add as many member accounts as possible during the two-week training period after activating Detective, since Detective needs at least two weeks of data to build an accurate behavioral baseline — more accounts and history improve the fidelity of anomaly detection used in investigations. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/detective/latest/userguide/detective-data-training-period.html)
- **[multi-account at scale]** Use AWS Organizations integration to centrally manage the behavior graph across up to 1,200 member accounts, and designate a Detective delegated administrator account for the organization — avoids manual, error-prone per-account invitation management as your estate grows. [doc](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/security/simplify-setup-of-amazon-detective-with-aws-organizations/)
- **[multi-account at scale]** Turn on "Automatically enable new organization accounts" so newly created organization accounts are enrolled as Detective member accounts without manual action — closes coverage gaps for accounts created after initial setup. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/detective/latest/userguide/accounts-orgs-members.html)
- **[account moves]** Before removing an account from an organization, invite it to the target organization's behavior graph in advance — preserves investigative visibility during the transition, since leaving the source organization removes the account from the source's organization behavior graph. [doc](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/mt/aws-organizations-moving-an-organization-member-account-to-another-organization-part-3/)
- **[EKS/container coverage]** Enable the Amazon EKS audit logs optional data source (enabled by default for graphs created after July 26, 2022) so Detective can correlate Kubernetes control-plane activity with CloudTrail and VPC Flow Log data — without it, EKS clusters, pods, and container images have reduced investigative context. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/detective/latest/userguide/source-data-types-EKS.html)

## 💰 Cost Optimization
- **[usage visibility]** Use the Detective console's Usage page to track ingested data volume and projected 30-day cost by data source and by account — lets administrator and member accounts forecast spend before it accumulates unexpectedly. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/detective/latest/userguide/tracking-usage-logging.html)
- **[free trial]** Use the 30-day free trial (granted per account/Region when Detective is first enabled, and separately for optional data sources such as EKS audit logs) to validate value and estimate ongoing costs before committing — avoid enabling data sources broadly without reviewing trial-period usage estimates first. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/detective/latest/userguide/tracking-usage-logging.html)
- **[optional data sources]** Disable optional data sources you do not need — Amazon EKS audit logs and AWS Security Hub findings are both optional and can be turned off if you do not need that investigative coverage — since Detective charges per GB ingested per account/Region/month. [doc](https://aws.amazon.com/detective/faqs/)
- **[shared VPC]** Review projected costs carefully in shared VPC environments, since a Detective member account with a shared VPC causes Detective to monitor and charge for traffic from all accounts using that shared VPC, not just its own — plan behavior-graph membership and shared-VPC ownership together to avoid unexpected ingestion volume. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/detective/latest/userguide/usage-projected-cost-calculation.html)

## ⚙️ Operational Excellence
- **[investigation workflow]** Start multi-finding investigations from Detective finding groups rather than individual findings — threat actors typically generate multiple related findings over time, and finding groups (with generative-AI summaries) surface the root cause faster than reviewing findings one at a time. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/detective/latest/userguide/what-is-detective.html)
- **[investigation workflow]** Use Detective Investigations to check IAM users and IAM roles for indicators of compromise (IOCs) as a triage step — focuses analyst effort on the most critical, machine-learning-flagged issues instead of manually reviewing every finding. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/detective/latest/userguide/what-is-detective.html)
- **[multi-account]** Manage member account invitations and organization enrollment from a single delegated administrator account, and use the provided Python scripts for bulk account management operations — reduces manual, per-account operational overhead at scale. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/detective/latest/userguide/accounts.html)
- **[cross-service pivoting]** Pair Detective with GuardDuty and Security Hub so analysts can pivot directly from a finding to its Detective profile — shortens the path from detection to root-cause investigation. [doc](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/security/simplify-setup-of-amazon-detective-with-aws-organizations/)
- **[deep log access]** Integrate Detective with Amazon Security Lake to query and retrieve the underlying raw CloudTrail, VPC Flow Log, and EKS audit log data behind a finding — gives investigators the ability to drill into raw events beyond Detective's own visualizations. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/detective/latest/userguide/what-is-detective.html)
- **[network investigation]** Use Detective's VPC flow volume visualizations to examine EC2 instance and Kubernetes pod network activity for a selected time range — helps validate security group behavior and diagnose anomalous traffic without building custom log queries. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/detective/latest/userguide/what-is-detective.html)

# Amazon CloudWatch — Best Practices

## Common scenarios
- Centralizing metrics, logs, and alarms for EC2/ECS/EKS/Lambda workloads        → Operational Excellence, Reliability
- Detecting sensitive data (PII) and controlling access to log data        → Security
- Reducing observability spend at scale (log retention, EMF, high-resolution metrics)        → Cost Optimization
- Monitoring and alerting across multiple accounts and Regions        → Operational Excellence, Reliability

## 🔒 Security
- **[IAM]** Start from AWS managed policies and move to least-privilege customer managed policies scoped to specific CloudWatch actions and resources — avoids over-broad access to metrics, logs, and alarms. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AmazonCloudWatch/latest/monitoring/security_iam_id-based-policy-examples.html)
- **[IAM]** Add policy conditions (e.g., require SSL, restrict by source service) and validate policies with IAM Access Analyzer — catches overly permissive or non-functional statements before they're attached. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AmazonCloudWatch/latest/monitoring/security_iam_id-based-policy-examples.html)
- **[IAM]** Require multi-factor authentication (MFA) for accounts that can modify or delete CloudWatch resources — reduces blast radius of credential compromise. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AmazonCloudWatch/latest/monitoring/data-protection.html)
- **[Log data]** Enable CloudWatch Logs data protection policies to automatically detect and mask sensitive data (PII, credentials, financial data) as it's ingested — prevents sensitive information from being exposed in plaintext logs and downstream consumers. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AmazonCloudWatch/latest/logs/mask-sensitive-log-data.html)
- **[Log data]** Never put confidential or sensitive information into tags or free-form text fields (such as log group/stream names) — this data can surface in billing records and diagnostic logs outside your control. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AmazonCloudWatch/latest/monitoring/data-protection.html)
- **[Encryption]** Encrypt log groups with AWS KMS customer managed keys in addition to default encryption at rest — supports stricter compliance and key-rotation requirements. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AmazonCloudWatch/latest/logs/encrypt-log-data-kms.html)
- **[Governance]** Use a service control policy (SCP) to deny `logs:DeleteLogGroup` and `logs:PutRetentionPolicy` except for a designated provisioning role — prevents accidental or malicious deletion/shortening of retention on critical log groups. [doc](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/machine-learning/detect-and-protect-sensitive-data-with-amazon-lex-and-amazon-cloudwatch-logs/)
- **[Transport]** Use TLS 1.2 or later (TLS 1.3 recommended) for all communication with CloudWatch APIs, and enable AWS CloudTrail to log API and user activity against CloudWatch resources — provides an audit trail for configuration changes. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AmazonCloudWatch/latest/logs/data-protection.html)
- **[Network]** Use interface VPC endpoints (AWS PrivateLink) for CloudWatch, CloudWatch Synthetics, and CloudWatch Network Monitoring to keep traffic between your VPC and CloudWatch off the public internet. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AmazonCloudWatch/latest/monitoring/cloudwatch-and-interface-VPC.html)

## 🛡️ Reliability
- **[Alarms]** Adopt CloudWatch's built-in best-practice alarm recommendations for AWS service metrics, including suggested thresholds, instead of defining thresholds from scratch — reduces the chance of missing critical monitoring coverage. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AmazonCloudWatch/latest/monitoring/Best-Practice-Alarms.html)
- **[Alarms]** Use composite alarms to aggregate related metric alarms (e.g., CPU + memory, or all alarms for one application) into a single Boolean-logic alarm — cuts alarm fatigue by notifying teams once per incident instead of once per underlying alarm. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AmazonCloudWatch/latest/monitoring/alarm-combining.html)
- **[Alarms]** Configure actions (SNS notification, Lambda, Systems Manager OpsItem/Incident Manager) only on the composite or aggregated alarm level where possible, leaving underlying metric alarms action-free — avoids duplicate/conflicting notifications during widespread issues. [doc](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/mt/automating-amazon-cloudwatch-alarms-with-aws-systems-manager/)
- **[EMF alarms]** When alarming on embedded metric format (EMF) metrics, flush logs at an interval of 5 seconds or less (or use "M out of N" alarm evaluation for platforms where flush interval isn't controllable) — avoids alarming on partial or missing data points. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AmazonCloudWatch/latest/monitoring/CloudWatch_Embedded_Metric_Format_Alarms.html)
- **[Design]** Design logging/monitoring coverage around all six areas of the observability lifecycle — ingestion, search/analysis, metrics/alarming, availability monitoring, tracing, and dashboards — so gaps in one area don't blind you to failures. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/prescriptive-guidance/latest/implementing-logging-monitoring-cloudwatch/welcome.html)
- **[Resilience assessment]** Use AWS Resilience Hub to generate CloudWatch alarm recommendations (including RTO/RPO-related alarms) tied to your application's actual resources, and re-run assessments after architecture changes to keep alarms current. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/resilience-hub/latest/userguide/alarms.html)

## ⚡ Performance Efficiency
- **[Metrics]** Use high-resolution (1-second) custom metrics only where sub-minute granularity is operationally necessary — standard 1-minute resolution is sufficient for most metrics and avoids the added cost and volume of frequent `PutMetricData` calls. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AmazonCloudWatch/latest/monitoring/publishingMetrics.html)
- **[Custom metrics]** Prefer the CloudWatch embedded metric format (EMF) over direct `PutMetricData` calls for ephemeral or high-throughput sources such as Lambda and containers — metrics are extracted asynchronously from logs, avoiding synchronous API calls and enabling deep root-cause queries via Logs Insights on the same data. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AmazonCloudWatch/latest/monitoring/CloudWatch_Embedded_Metric_Format.html)
- **[Custom metrics]** Avoid metric filters or EMF dimensions built from high-cardinality values (such as request IDs) — each unique dimension combination creates a distinct custom metric, degrading both cost and usability. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AmazonCloudWatch/latest/monitoring/CloudWatch_Embedded_Metric_Format.html)
- **[Logs Insights]** Use the `pattern` and on-demand `anomaly` commands in CloudWatch Logs Insights for ad hoc investigation of unusual log behavior instead of manually scanning log streams — surfaces frequency changes, new patterns, and token variations automatically. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AmazonCloudWatch/latest/logs/LogsAnomalyDetection-Insights.html)

## 💰 Cost Optimization
- **[Retention]** Set an explicit retention policy on every log group (rather than relying on the "never expire" default) sized to actual operational, compliance, and troubleshooting needs — the default indefinite retention accumulates unnecessary storage costs indefinitely. [doc](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/compute/optimizing-your-aws-lambda-costs-part-2/)
- **[Retention]** Use a tiered retention strategy: short retention (7–30 days) in source/workload accounts for immediate operational needs, longer retention (90+ days) only in a centralized/compliance account — avoids paying long-term storage costs on every copy of the data. [doc](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/mt/simplifying-log-management-using-amazon-cloudwatch-logs-centralization/)
- **[Archival]** Export logs that must be kept for months or years for compliance to Amazon S3 (with lower-cost storage classes) instead of retaining them indefinitely in CloudWatch Logs — CloudWatch Logs storage pricing doesn't decrease as data ages. [doc](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/compute/optimizing-your-aws-lambda-costs-part-2/)
- **[Automation]** Automate retention policy enforcement across new and existing log groups (for example, with EventBridge + Lambda reacting to log group creation) so retention settings don't depend on manual follow-up. [doc](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/infrastructure-and-automation/reduce-log-storage-costs-by-automating-retention-settings-in-amazon-cloudwatch/)
- **[Ingestion]** Prefer embedded metric format (EMF) over per-datapoint `PutMetricData` calls for custom metrics — consolidating metric emission into log writes can substantially cut custom-metric costs versus one API call per data point. [doc](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/mt/lowering-costs-and-focusing-on-our-customers-with-amazon-cloudwatch-embedded-custom-metrics/)
- **[Centralization]** When centralizing logs cross-account/cross-Region, use log group filters to centralize only the applications that need it, and reuse source accounts as backup copies where possible — avoids paying for additional copies ($0.05/GB) of data that doesn't need duplication. [doc](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/mt/simplifying-log-management-using-amazon-cloudwatch-logs-centralization/)
- **[Ingestion]** If ingestion costs from vended logs (e.g., S3 access logs) are higher than expected, reduce retention and reassess which source buckets/resources truly need CloudWatch Logs delivery — volume-based pricing means unnecessary sources add up quickly. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AmazonS3/latest/userguide/sal-cw-troubleshooting.html)

## ⚙️ Operational Excellence
- **[Multi-account]** Use CloudWatch cross-account observability (Observability Access Manager) to set up a central monitoring account that can search, visualize, and correlate metrics, logs, and traces from multiple source accounts without assuming roles — reduces the manual effort of switching accounts during incident response. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AmazonCloudWatch/latest/monitoring/CloudWatch-Cross-Account-Methods.html)
- **[Multi-account]** Choose the right cross-account mechanism for the need — cross-account observability for unified telemetry access, cross-account cross-Region console/dashboards for navigation without data pipelines, or cross-account cross-Region centralization when you need consolidated copies of logs/metrics in one destination. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AmazonCloudWatch/latest/monitoring/CloudWatch-Cross-Account-Methods.html)
- **[Dashboards]** Build cross-account, cross-Region dashboards with one-click drill-down into account/Region-specific detail views instead of requiring on-call engineers to log in and out of multiple accounts during an incident. [doc](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/aws/cross-account-cross-region-dashboards-with-amazon-cloudwatch/)
- **[Alarm workflow]** Use the CloudWatch console's alarm recommendations to pre-fill thresholds and export infrastructure-as-code alarm definitions (CloudFormation, CLI, Terraform) instead of hand-authoring every alarm — keeps alarm configuration consistent and reviewable in version control. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AmazonCloudWatch/latest/monitoring/Best-Practice-Alarms.html)
- **[Deployment planning]** Plan your CloudWatch deployment around the number of accounts/Regions, workload variety, and centralization needs up front, and review/update the logging and monitoring approach whenever workloads change — an ad hoc approach leads to inconsistent agent configuration and coverage gaps. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/prescriptive-guidance/latest/implementing-logging-monitoring-cloudwatch/planning-cloudwatch-deployment.html)
- **[Metric filters]** Use metric filters to convert recurring log patterns (such as `ERROR` counts) into first-class CloudWatch metrics with dimensions, rather than querying raw logs repeatedly for the same operational signal. [doc](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/AmazonCloudWatch/latest/logs/FilterAndPatternSyntaxForMetricFilters.html)
